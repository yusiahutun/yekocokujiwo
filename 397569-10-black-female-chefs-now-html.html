1. Sam Davis

Many people know the astounding Sam Davis by her company name; Savor by Sam. The gorgeous African American chef started her company in 2011. She has since made food for the who is who in the entertainment industry and corporate America.

Sam is among the female chefs on TV you have to watch. Before becoming a full-time chef, she had a medical career in cancer research. Some of the big names she has catered for include:

  • The Daily Show
  • Adidas
  • The US Open
  • Leslie Jones
  • The teams at Roc Nation

2. Petrina Peart

Petrina Peart is never shy talking about her Jamaican roots. The incredible chef was born in Jamaica but moved to the United States with her family when she was young. Not many people know that chef Petrina spent about a decade in the Air Force.

Her experience in the Air Force influenced her passion for food as she realized how food is a unifying factor whenever she travelled. Petrina is making moves in the Vegas food scene and being appreciated for her work. Many people who choose the vegan lifestyle enjoy her cooking and hacks in the kitchen.

3. Chaya Fletcher

Chaya Fletcher is not just a chef, but also a successful entrepreneur and educator for Oklahoma City Public Schools. Fletcher’s wondrous work has enabled her to work with celebrity chefs like Kurt Fleisfresser, Rachel Ray, Rick Bayless, and Dean Fearing.

Other than being the culinary queen in her city, she also serves as an advocate for women of colour through the Board of Directors of OKC Artists for Justice. Chaya’s role allows her to spread awareness on issues affecting her community and especially black women.

4. Airis Johnson

Airis Johnson’s dream was to run a Fortune 500 company after finishing her marketing major at Southern University. As fate would, however, have it, she fell in love with food and cooking after making a simple meal in her college dorm room.

Her friends enjoyed the food, and that was all she needed to take an interest in the culinary world. Today, Airis is one of the celebrated African American female chefs in New York where she resides. She has worked with some of the biggest names in the culinary world, like:

5. Tirzah Love

Tirzah Love is one of the most celebrated black female chefs and the owner of Tirzah Catering LLC. Her life in the culinary industry started with a small blog she created to offer catering services to small parties, baby showers, and weddings.

Social media played a massive role in Tirzah's career as she started getting multiple requests for her services. Today, she is one of the celebrated female chefs in America, thanks to her phenomenal cooking skills. You can get her services as a private chef or catch her during her cooking lessons, Cooking with Tirzah.

6. Nyesha Arrington

Nyesha Arrington knew she wanted to be a chef ever since she was a teenager. Her multicultural background also shaped her passion as her grandmother is Korean while her grandfather is of black and Cherokee descent. She is among the black female chefs on food network whose work speaks for them.

7. Avanelle James

Avanelle James is without a doubt one of the chefs whose cooking you want to taste. She is one of the most famous black female bakers in America. She has worked in high-end hotels including:

  • The Hilton in Trinidad
  • Crews Inn Yachting Centre
  • Hyatt Regency Trinidad

Avanelle James won the Pastry Chef Competition Bronze award during the CHTA's "Taste of the Caribbean" Culinary Competition in 2009. She came second in the Trinidad and Tobago Hospitality Institute Iron Chef competition in 2003.

8. Mashama Bailey

Mashama Bailey grew up moving between New York City and Georgia. She believed that the world had much more to offer, and that is why she started experimenting with her cooking skills. She trained with the French cooking teacher Anne Willan.

9. Jennifer Hill Booker

Jennifer Hill Booker takes pride as an executive chef, cookbook author, educator, and TV personality. The remarkable chef has worked with distinguished chefs like her both in her country and internationally.

She has also collaborated with many brands to bring people healthy and spicy dining habits. Jennifer Hill Booker is the founder of Southern Divas of the New South Dinner Series and a member of Les Dames d’Escoffier International.

10. Nina Compton

Nina Compton grew up in St. Lucia. As she moved around various kitchens in the Caribbean, she realized that many of the kitchens were white, masculine, and very French. That pushed her to take professional cooking seriously and become one of the renowned female black chefs in the world. She can prepare the finest European and Caribbean dishes you have ever tasted.
