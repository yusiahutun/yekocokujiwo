'I still struggle with food': Iron Man actor Ashley Hamilton breaks his silence on his longtime fight with eating disorders

Ashley Hamilton has battled the demons of drugs and alcohol, but believes he had previous underlying disorders, too.

The 38-year-old actor who has a small role in the blockbuster Iron Man 3 movie has spoken publicly for the first time about his ongoing struggle with eating disorders.

The son of actor George Hamilton has been sober for more than six years and has seen his career get back on track while seeking treatment within the past year for his eating issues.

Eating disorder: Ashley Hamilton, shown in April at the Iron Man 3 premiere, recently told People that he suffered from bulimia and anorexia

'I suffered from bulimia and anorexia,' he recently told People. 'I believe it was my problem before I got into drugs and alcohol. I used the drugs and alcohol to control the food addiction.'

Ashley said his eating disorder continues to be a struggle.

'I don't have it perfect now. I've been free from bulimia for years but I still struggle with food, restricting or overeating. That's been the hardest for me in sobriety,' he said.

Longtime disorder: Ashley, shown with father George Hamilton in October 2008, said his issues with eating disorders came before his struggles with alcohol and drugs

Ashley told the magazine his nickname was Fatley at Beverly Hills High School and he's heard from others who have found it tough to talk about their eating disorders.

'It's almost like drug addiction is totally acceptable to talk about in Hollywood. But food addiction? Nobody wants to talk about that,' he said. 'It's really shameful as a man to have that.'

The six-foot, three-inch actor said his weight can fluctuate between 180 to 250 pounds and he's surrounded by food constantly.

Whirlwind romance: Ashley married Shannon Doherty in October 1993 after they knew each other for two weeks and they are shown here that year at Los Angeles International Airport

'There are so many people in our country suffering from this. It's an emotional problem and that's been the struggle for me through sobriety,' he said.

Hamilton gave his longtime friend Robert Downey Jr. credit for helping him get a gig on the new Iron Man movie.

'I just need somebody to give me the opportunity to trust in me sober-wise and give me a shot of this magnitude,' he said. 'I'll forever owe him for that.'

Ashley also has a song called Let's Go All The Way on the Iron Man 3 soundtrack with his band The Wondergirls and friend Robbie Williams.
