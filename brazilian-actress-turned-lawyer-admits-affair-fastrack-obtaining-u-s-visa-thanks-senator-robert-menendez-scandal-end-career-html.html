Brazilian actress says she had affair with Senator Robert Menendez donor

June 26, 2024
blog

A Brazilian actress-turned-lawyer has conformed reports that she had an affair with a friend and top donor of New Jersey Senator Robert Menendez - but insists she had no idea the indicted lawmaker gave her special treatment to fastrack at US visa for her.

Juliana Lopes Leite defended herself last week during a prime-time interview on Brazilian TV, in response to a recent exposé detailed in the New York Post. 

Menendez was charged in April is now facing the possibility of 15 years in prison for using his position to help Dr Salomon Melgen, 60, in exchange for nearly $1million in gifts and campaign contributions. 

The 61-year-old Democrat is accused of using his influence to get visas for married Melgen's multiple young lovers abroad - including Leite, who posed on the cover of 'Sexy' magazine and a Ukrainian actress who moved to Florida to live in one of Melgen's homes.

Lopes Leite, 34, who works as lawyer in Miami told The Post last week the first she learned Menendez's intervention was after reading the federal indictment against him.

In the O Globo interview, she said her romance with Melgen began when she was 23 and living in the U.S. 

'We were together three or four months at that time,' she said.

After returning to Brazil, the model went on to take part in the Brazilian version of Big Brother, when out of the blue a few years later, Melgen got back in touch and gave her a phone call. 

'He said, "I want to make you a proposal: Would you be interested in making a specialty here in the US?"' Lopes Leite said.

She was given a visa to return to the U.S. a day after Senator Menendez office intervened, according to the Post.

The indictment says Menendez knew Leite was one of Melgen's mistresses when he agreed to help with her visa application, and ordered his adviser to write a letter in the woman's favor to the State Department. 

In the letter, his senior policy adviser wrote that 'Leite Lopes (no relation to me) has her visa application appointment in Brasilia, Brazil, tomorrow . . . Sen. Menendez would like to advocate unconditionally for Dr. Melgen and encourage careful consideration of [Girlfriend 1]'s visa application.'

The State Department got back to Menendez's office in just a few hours later and the following day Leite's visa was approved.

Melgen is now  behind bars and faces charges in the indictment unsealed in April, in addition to a separate 76-count indictment for Medicaid fraud. 

He remains behind bars at the Federal Detention Center in Miami after being deemed a flight risk. 

'He's the one who should be worried because he is behind bars, poor thing,' Lopes Leite said. 

Menendez and Melgen met at a fundraiser in 1993, shortly after Menendez was voted into the Senate.
