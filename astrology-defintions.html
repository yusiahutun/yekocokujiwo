Beginner's Guide to Astrology-Astrology Terms You Should Know

May 09, 2024
blog

Air signs: Libra, Aquarius and Gemini. People who are air signs tend to lead with their mind and appear detached, but are great communicators and are very flexible. 

Aquarius: People born between January 20-February 18 are independent, and are always thinking about the future.

Aries: People born between March 21-April 19 are bold, fearless  and take charge of any challenge they come across. 

Ascendent: The angle at which the next zodiac sign is approaching in the east at the specific time and location of an event. This concept is often used in the context of birth, by measuring the angle of the following zodiac, or "rising sign," in the east based on when and where a person is born. People's rising signs are usually connected to their upbringing, how they present themselves to the world, or how a person copes with uncomfortable situations. 

Aspect: The angle planets make with each other, creating a certain type of relationship or connection.

Birth chart: A diagram that represents the position of planets the moment a person was born. The planet's positioning is said to hold important information about a person's life. This is also referred to as a "natal chart." 

Cancer: People born between June 21-July 22 are sensitive, nurturing and make everyone feel at home.

Capricorn: People born between December 22-January 19 are determined, calm and make for great leaders. 

Cardinal signs: Aries, Cancer, Libra and Capricorn. These signs are associated with the start of a new season—spring, summer, fall and winter, respectively—and therefore signify new beginnings.

Configuration: A certain way planets are positioned that has a significant meaning. One example is the T-square configuration, which is when three planets form a right triangle shape in the sky. It indicates a struggle in life, but also creates a stronger drive to achieve. 

Earth signs: Capricorn, Taurus and Virgo. People who are earth signs tend to be "earthy," attentive to the five senses, and blend in with their natural habitat. But they can also be stubborn and workaholics. 

Eclipse: When the sun and the moon intersect in their paths around the earth. These include solar eclipses (when the moon casts a shadow on the earth) and lunar eclipses (when the earth casts a shadow on the moon). Eclipses bring about about a change in circumstance, and a series of them are usually tied together by a certain theme.

Equinox: When the center of the sun lines up with the Earth's equator, which signifies the beginning of spring and fall. The spring equinox, in March, is associated with new beginnings, while the autumn equinox in September is connected to gathering harvest and conserving your resources. 

Fire signs: Aries, Leo and Sagittarius. People who are fire signs tend to be drawn to sudden bursts of enthusiasm, seem passionate and inspired, and dislike mundanity. They are often intuitive, which can lead to overlooking details or not realizing the impact of their actions. 

Fixed signs: Taurus, Leo, Scorpio and Aquarius. These signs are associated with the middle of the seasons, and stand for stability and permanence.

Gemini: People born May 21-June 20 are curious, talkative and great communicators. 

Houses: A birth chart is divided into 12 discrete sections, called houses. However, there are conflicting views on on how these divisions should be made. Each house represents a different experience or energy. The planets' locations in the houses on a birth chart reveals how certain experiences will affect a person's life to what extent.

Jupiter: The planet associated with luck, benevolence, intellect and optimism. Leisure and wealth are also said to be ruled by Jupiter.

Leo: People born between July 23- August 22 are very outgoing, charming and their love of laughter makes them always fun to be around. 

Libra: People born between September 23-October 22 are very diplomatic, balanced and graceful.

Mercury: The planet associated with communication, logic, reasoning, restlessness, opinions and dexterity. When Mercury is in retrograde, it is said that our communications will be challenged. 

Mutable signs: Gemini, Virgo, Sagittarius and Pisces. These signs correspond with the ends of the seasons, signifying transition. 

Pisces: People born between February 19-March 20 are intuitive, compassionate which make them great listeners and shoulders to lean on. 

Planetary return: When a planet in transit returns to the position it was at when a person was born.

Retrograde: When a planet appears to be moving backwards in the sky. If a planet is in retrograde on your birth chart, that means you'll feel the energy portrayed by that planet in a deeper, more internal way. 

Sagittarius: People born between November 22-December 21 are outgoing, philosophical and will often give you brutally honest advice. 

Scorpio: People born between October 23- November 22 are passionate and intense with a magnetic personality that can draw anyone in. 

Solstice: The longest and shortest days of the year, when the sky reaches its highest or lowest points in the sky, respectively.  The summer solstice in June coincides with abundance and harvest, and the winter solstice in December, though a time of darkness, also gives way to new life. 

Taurus: People born between April 22-May 20 are stubborn, determined and have a penchant for the finer things in life. 

Transit: The study of where planets will be in the future and what events or energies their eventual positions will cause. Since planets are always in motion, astrologers pay close attention as they change sign, house and aspect as they travel. 

Uranus: This planet represents unexpected disruptions and sudden catastrophes.

Virgo: People born between August 23-September 22 are practical, loyal and their perfectionism makes them very detail-oriented.

Water Signs: Cancer, Scorpio and Pisces. People who are water signs tend to have waves of emotions, be sensitive in relationships and are able to bring people together. Sometimes they can absorb other people's "vibes" and therefore may have trouble creating personal boundaries.

Zodiac: A division of the ecliptic (the path of the sun) into twelve 30-degree segments we call "signs," each with its own set of characteristics, also known as sun sign.
