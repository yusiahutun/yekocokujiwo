The 12 Best Ammonia-Free Hair Dyes of 2024

May 22, 2024
blog

If you’ve ever gotten your hair colored or done a DIY dye job at home, chances are you’ve gotten a whiff of ammonia before. A common ingredient found in many hair dyes, ammonia is an active that makes strands more susceptible to color saturation. More specifically, celebrity hairstylist Michelle Cleveland says, “Ammonia raises the pH level of the hair during the oxidation process which in turn opens the cuticles of the hair, and allows the color to be deposited deep into the cortex of the hair.” In short: Ammonia has long been the key to, well, long-lasting color. 

Considering the strong scent and often irritating nature of the ingredient, though, scientists have set out to find ingredients, such as ethanolamine, monoethanolamine (MEA), and sodium carbonate, that mimic ammonia without the aroma. Because of this, ammonia-free hair dyes are rising in popularity, with many brands hopping onto the bandwagon. While it’s great to have variety, a highly-saturated market means that it can be difficult to know which dyes are actually worthwhile. To help clear up any confusion, we researched the highest-rated formulas and chatted with celebrity stylists for their top picks.

Ahead, uncover the best ammonia-free hair dyes that provide rich, vibrant color.

What to Keep in Mind

Ingredients

Hair dye is known to be drying. As such, Dorram says it’s wise to always look for formulas enriched with aloe vera, coconut oil, argan oil, and vitamin E to restore and maintain your hair’s hydration. Furthermore, Cleveland says to keep an eye out for formulas infused with natural keratin, as it will help strengthen strands during the dyeing process. Madison Reed Radiant Hair Color Kit contains both argan oil and keratin to simultaneously condition and strengthen hair during the dye process. 

Color Range

Many at-home hair dyes come in an impressive shade range — a majority on our list are offered in over 30 hues, with our best overall winner, Revlon Colorsilk Beautiful Color Permanent Hair Color, offering an impressive 41 options. While selecting a brand with a wider variety of color options may seem like the best thing to do, Dorram suggests first determining if you want to go lighter or darker, then look at the options. 

“When choosing a hair color, it's important to consider your skin tone and natural hair color to find a shade that will complement your features,” says Korab. “It's also a good idea to do a strand test before coloring your entire head to ensure that the color turns out as you expected.” Building off of this, Dorram also recommends sticking with a color that is either two shades lighter or darker than your current color for the best results. 

If you’re looking for a more fun color, like blue or pink, we recommend Arctic Fox Semi-Permanent Hair Color or Hally Color Cloud.

Dye Type

Ammonia-free hair dyes exist across three categories: Permanent, demi-permanent, and semi-permanent. According to Korab, permanent hair color typically uses a developer in place of ammonia to activate the color. These dyes, like Herbatint Permanent Haircolor Gel, are designed to last the longest.

Demi- and semi-permanent dyes are shorter-lived. “Demi-permanents require a mixing agent (developer) to gently push the color slightly under the cuticle for some gray coverage; semi-permanent hair dye, however, does not require a mixing agent and typically only lasts about four to 12 shampoos before fading,” Cleveland explains. Additionally, she points out that both semi- and demi-permanent dyes only deposit color. “Meaning they will only make hair darker,” she says. “Typically, they will only cover gray hair up to 70 percent.”

Your Questions, Answered

Are ammonia-free hair dyes better for hair?

They can be. “Ammonia-free hair dyes can be better for you if you have a sensitive scalp, skin, or eyes,” Doram says. Additionally, she says that if you’re sensitive to scents, ammonia-free hair color is less pungent, and thus more tolerable. 

It comes with its drawbacks, though. “If you are trying to lift and deposit color onto the hair, like in a gray coverage situation, an ammonia-based color is ideal for the best coverage,” celebrity stylist and #Mydentity founder, Guy Tang, says. “If you want a natural grow out, then an ammonia-free hair color would be best; or if you’re looking for a temporary color.”

Are ammonia-free dyes effective at covering grays?

While certain ammonia-free dyes will cover gray (like Naturtint Permanent Hair Color), Tang admits that he finds ammonia-based color to be the most effective. “Especially when covering coarse gray hair,” he exclaims. That said, he admits that ammonia-free hair color works well for blending gray hair.

Is ammonia-free hair dye safe to use during pregnancy?

While ammonia-free hair dye is thought to be gentler and ultimately safe to use during pregnancy as a result, all of the experts we spoke with recommend consulting your physician before dyeing your hair while pregnant.
