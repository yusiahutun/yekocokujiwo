The manager of B.B. King, who died last night, today hit back at claims by his daughter that she had denied her a last chance of seeing her ailing father.

Shirley King, 65, vented her anger online against Laverne Toney, the blues' legend's long time manager, saying that she had been 'hiding something' and that she was not telling her where her father's body had been taken.

King died last night at his home a few miles from the Las Vegas strip with his housekeeper by his side.

But Toney told Daily Mail Online she was surprised to learn about B.B. King's daughter's rant and insisted she had not heard from her.
