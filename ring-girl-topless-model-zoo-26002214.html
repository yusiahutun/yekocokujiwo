UFC ring girl was topless model for Zoo magazine and co-wrote song with Gary Barlow

February 04, 2024
blog

Brit Carly Baker is far from being just the UFC's first European ring girl, having had a variety of job roles which has included co-writing a song with Gary Barlow.

The 34-year-old, who was born in Wimbledon, London, is well known to MMA fans for strutting her stuff around the Octagon.

She mostly appears at overseas events for Dana White's American promotion, and featured during their 'Fight Island' cards in Abu Dhabi during the height of the pandemic.

Her modelling career began 20 years ago, though, at the age of just 14.

Carly appeared in numerous print publications as a teenager while also starting her music career.

She first sang in a pop duo called 'Mynxters', who were managed by Japanese record company Fuji International and released the single 'Hello Kitty' in 2001.

Then she went on to sign with BMG records and became a part of the girl band 'SW1'.

The company had Take That chief Barlow on their books as well as rock band Kasabian and American music legend Usher.

Carly's signing career didn't take off, but she did find considerable success in pageantry.

She represented her hometown as a beauty queen for Miss Great Britain before going on to have a photo shoot with lads mag Zoo in 2013.

Since then she has appeared in TV adverts for betting company Coral and cameoed in Professor Green's 'I need you tonight' video.

Carly will be no doubt be back working with the UFC when they return to the UK later this year as she wowed fans at the event in 2017.

It was announced earlier this week that UFC London will be taking place in March, three years since the promotion's last visit across the pond.

A number of Britain's top fighters are expected to throw down, with the likes of Paddy Pimblett and Molly McCann already confirmed for the card.
