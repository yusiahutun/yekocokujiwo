Iconic reggae superstar Freddie McGregor is a proud father after his son's Chino performance of his songs in a recent concert.

The celebrated singer was overjoyed when he shared the clip on his Facebook page. He congratulated his son for an 'outstanding performance.'

He wrote:

"My son Chino McGregor live on Onstage with Qyor performing my classic songs I See It In You, Prophecy, Push Come To Shove, Let Him Try and I Will Wait For You. This is an outstanding performance."

Freddie McGregor's recent health scare

The 66-year-old renowned singer began singing when he was only seven years old, and his music is loved across different generations and nations.

Unfortunately, on November 28, 2022, he suffered a stroke, and doctors recommended he rests. This inevitably caused the UB40 Tour, which he was a part of, to be postponed to an unspecified date.

Like father like son

Daniel McGregor, better known as Chino, rose in 2010 because of his global style of dancehall.

He is a grammy award-winning songwriter, and fans agreed that he sounded exactly like his father during his latest performance of his dad's songs.

As others wished the older McGregor a quick recovery and encouraged him to rest, they said he shouldn't worry as Chino on stage is just as good as he is, and if it wasn't for their age difference, people couldn't tell the difference.

Magoha's son followed in his footsteps

Time and time again, children who have followed in their parent's footsteps continually prove that the apple never falls far from the tree.

George Albert Omore Magoha wore many hats in his lifetime. He was a Urologist, consultant surgeon, academic administrator and served as a Professor of Surgery at Maseno University School of Medicine in Kisumu. His most memorable position was when he served as the Cabinet secretary of Education.

Magoha's only child Michael followed in his late dad's footsteps career-wise. He studied in Kenya's finest schools, first at Strathmore primary school, then proceeded to Brookhouse.

Michael is an employee at the University of Nairobi as a neurosurgeon and lecturer.
