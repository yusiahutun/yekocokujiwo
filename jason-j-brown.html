Jason J Brown - Biography

blog

    Who is Jason J Brown?

    Jason Paul "J" Brown, the English singer and rapper, gained recognition as the lead singer of the internationally successful boy band Five. Born on 13 June 1976 at the Cambridge Military Hospital in Aldershot, J Brown's upbringing was marked by his father's military service, leading to a childhood spent in various locations, including Germany, Canada, and different parts of northern England. 

    However, it was his time in Warrington and Newton-le-Willows that shaped his teenage and young adult years before his journey with Five began.

    His career

    As a member of Five, J Brown experienced immense fame and achieved remarkable success. Over a span of five years, the band sold more than 20 million records worldwide. However, following the group's disbandment in late 2001, Brown maintained a low profile, occasionally participating in protests against UK involvement in Middle East wars.

    In 2006, J Brown made a notable comeback when he appeared as a contestant on the seventh series of ITV's popular show, I'm a Celebrity...Get Me Out of Here! Brown's resilience and charm led him to the final, finishing in third place alongside Christopher Biggins and Janice Dickinson. 

    Following his success on the show, Brown continued to make television appearances, joining Ant McPartlin's team on Ant & Dec's Saturday Night Takeaway and featuring on Fiver's Generation Sex. He also explored his talents as a writer and producer for other musicians.

    His personal life

    After initial discussions with his former bandmates, J Brown and Five announced their reunion for the ITV2 series The Big Reunion. However, just before the show's launch, Brown made the difficult decision to step away from the public eye, opting out of the reunion. 

    Despite his absence during the first series of The Big Reunion, Brown made a poignant appearance in the second season, sharing his perspective in a right-to-reply interview and revealing his personal struggles, including a past suicide attempt. 

    He acknowledged his behavior might have been perceived as overbearing but denied allegations of bullying. Brown made it clear that he had moved on from his time with Five and no longer felt comfortable returning to the band.

    Outside of his music career, J Brown's personal life includes a past on-off relationship with Spice Girls singer Melanie C. As he continues to navigate his path away from the public eye, Brown focuses on personal growth and new endeavors.
