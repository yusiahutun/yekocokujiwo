Michigan supporters toasted a first National Championship since 1997 by pouring on to the streets, burning couches and singing Mr Brightside back in Ann Arbor.

Jim Harbaugh's players, meanwhile, celebrated their victory over Washington by taking National Championship chairs home from NRG Stadium.

The Wolverines were crowned the best team in college football on Monday night after beating the Huskies 34-13 in Houston.

Shortly after the final whistle, clips started to emerge on social media showing wild celebrations both in the stadium and back on campus.

One video showed a large crowd gathered around a couch, with fans filming on their phones as one student in Michican merch sets fire to the furniture.

'It's great to be a Michigan Wolverine!' the fans chanted before cheers went up as the couch went up in flames.

The furniture was then flipped over, causing panic among some of those at the front of the crowd.

At the nearby Crisler Center, Michigan hosted a free, student-only watch party for Monday's game. Clips from during the game showed fans in the stands celebrating a Wolverines touchdown as the action unfolded on the big screens.

Later on, however, the crowd had descended on the court below and were singing the team's unofficial anthem 'Mr Brightside' by The Killers.

Outside on the streets on Ann Arbor, supporters gathered late into the night, with pictures showing them climbing trees - and even carrying them - following the win.

Back in Houston, meanwhile, some Michigan players left NRG stadium carrying leather chairs which read: 'National Championship.'

'We got a chair, baby!'' one shouted before adding: 'And I guess we won the "Natty" too!'
