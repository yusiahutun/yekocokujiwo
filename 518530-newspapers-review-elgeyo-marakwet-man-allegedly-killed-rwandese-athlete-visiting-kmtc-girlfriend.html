On Wednesday, August 23, the daily newspapers reported on the preliminary findings in the killing of an athlete in Iten in a suspected love triangle.

The papers also reported on the appointment of Humphrey Wattanga Mulongo as the new Commissioner General of the Kenya Revenue Authority.

1. The Standard

This newspaper reports about the death of a Rwandanese athlete, and preliminary investigations now show that he was embroiled in a love triangle with the prime suspect in his death.

Rubayita Siragi, 34, had been in the country since 2017 and had been training at the Asic training camp in Iten until he met his death a few days ago.

3. Taifa Leo

This newspaper reports about Mombasa governor Abdullswamad Sheriff Nassir's narration of his supposed handshake with President William Ruto after the 2022 elections.

While speaking to concerned Mombasa ODM supporters who were sceptical about his ties with the president, Nassir said he and the president held a closed-door meeting, culminating in a handshake last year.

He said the meeting happened right before he and the president took their respective oaths of office, and they both agreed to work together.

"There was someone who was supporting us both and he helped bring me together with the president and that was the first time he and I shook hands after a long time," he said.

4. The Star

In this paper, residents of Karati in Naivasha have been left in shock and fear after a man took his own life over a Sacco loan that he guaranteed a colleague.

The man is said to have harmed himself to evade paying the colleague's KSh 500,000 loan, which he was required to repay to the Sacco after the debtor absconded payment.

In a note, he indicated he could not bring himself to repay a loan that had not benefited him.

It is alleged that the deceased's colleague wooed him into being his guarantor for the loan before disappearing, leaving him to bear the consequences.

5. People Daily

President William Ruto has appointed Humphrey Wattanga Mulongo as the new Commissioner General of the Kenya Revenue Authority, People Daily reports.

In a gazette notice dated Tuesday, August 22, Treasury Cabinet Secretary Njuguna Ndung'u said Mulongo will serve for a period of three years, effective August 22, 2023.
