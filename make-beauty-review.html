Make Beauty skincare and make-up review

April 25, 2024
blog

In our new regular feature #TheBrand, Bazaar’s digital beauty director looks into an exciting and efficacious brand taking the beauty industry by storm. This month it’s Make, the multifunctional make-up and skincare label taking a slower approach to beauty, avoiding the eternal treadmill of trends.

What's the story?

Make, developed and manufactured in the brand's home of New York City, is an edit of minimalist make-up and skincare essentials with a universal focus. "We try to create products that speak to individuals, regardless of their race, ethnicity, gender or age," co-founder and creative director Ariana Mouyiaris tells Bazaar. "We believe that people who are drawn to our approach and aesthetic connect to the idea that beauty starts from the inside out; that make-up and skincare shouldn’t be dictated by companies, but explored as part of an evolving, non-static identity."

For this reason, they avoid relentless trend-based launches, and take a slightly alternative approach outside of the traditional beauty sphere. "We’re trying to move away from the incessant fashion calendar and are looking to what we see as 'slow beauty'. Like 'slow food' or 'slow fashion': it’s inspired by each individual and their personal style."

Nuanced and changeable, Mouyiaris feels the 'slow beauty' movement is driven by self-expression, not seasonal releases. As a result, Make offers essentials with timeless appeal and versatile, multifunctional attributes for a personal expression.

How 'universal' is it?

MAKE Universal Stick

MAKE Universal Stick

All the colour cosmetics are positioned as shade-inclusive. "Since the brand’s inception, we have made a point to address as wide a range of skin tones as possible, and many of our products – Face Gloss, Dew Pot, Universal Stick, Bisou Bisou – are universal in tone," Mouyiaris tells us. "My father worked in the beauty industry, and he developed some of the first foundation shade ranges which accommodated people of all skin tones. From an early age, I was in the lab with him, and that approach has remained at the core of how I work." While Make's Soft Focus Foundation comes in 16 shades (far fewer than offerings commonly being released by big beauty brands nowadays), they range in description from being 'for very fair complexions with yellow undertones', to 'for very deep complexions with rich red undertones'.

When it comes to skincare, the streamlined offering includes products that would appeal to most skin types, though your complexion and personal preference will be the judge of that. We recommend the truly suit-all Dual-Phase Eye Make-up Remover and Succulent Skin Gel.

Where can we shop it?

The brand was born in 2013, but only launched in the UK at the end of 2018. Mouyiaris explains: "Make has always had an engaged and supportive audience in the UK – on the site, on social media, and through Net-a-Porter, which stocks our products. All of which led us to choose Selfridges to introduce our products on the ground." She now splits her time between New York and London.

"I think the UK has a very particular approach to beauty; people here are eager to try something different – something that breaks away from traditional ideas. That fits very naturally with our approach." As well as Net-a-Porter and Selfridges, there are some select products available on Feelunique.

What else should we know?

Make products are cruelty-free and many are vegan. The formulas are free of parabens. The brand is also socially minded.

Mouyiaris tells us that 10 per cent of sales are donated to the We See Beauty Foundation, which supports women-led, worker-owned cooperative businesses across the Americas. "When building the brand, we felt that being committed to small, grassroots, incremental change was part of our bottom line. It's not a token thing – it's not 10 per cent of after-sale profits, or a percentage of one lipstick, for one month. It's a consistent effort that we are committed to, year on year, and it’s part and parcel of who we are."
