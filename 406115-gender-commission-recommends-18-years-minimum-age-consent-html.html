Gender Commission Recommends 18 Years as Minimum Age of Consent

July 05, 2024

- A report developed by Plan International Kenya and the National Gender and Equality Commission puts 18 as the minimum age of consent towards carnal knowledge

- Its implementation aims to protect children as they are not mature enough to make decisions physically, socially and psychologically

- The report came into the fore two years since the Court of Appeal proposed a law change to lower the age of consent from 18 to 16

The legally accepted age of consent towards having carnal knowledge of an individual in Kenya is 18 years. This is after the launch of a report developed by Plan International Kenya in partnership with the National Gender and Equality Commission (NGEC).

According to Plan International - Kenya Country Director Kate Maina, the report exists to protect and advance the rights of girls and boys from being taken advantage of.

“We call out for partnerships in the establishment of child protection units in all counties in Kenya, where girls and boys in contact and conflict with the law receive the care and support needed through their involvement in court proceedings,” said Kate Maina.

She added that all stakeholders should ensure that appropriate information and knowledge, both formal and informal, is disseminated to adolescent girls and boys in and out of schools.

Kate also commended the ministry of labour and social protection for the successful submission of the Children’s Bill 2020 to the cabinet.

In attendance was Cabinet Secretary for Ministry of Labour and Social Protection Simon K. Chelugui, who alluded to the Kenyan law, which stipulates that a child is an individual who has not attained the age of eighteen years.

"I would strongly recommend that the age of consent should not be lowered at all, but measures should be put in place to mitigate the predicaments where young boys, as opposed to girls, are imprisoned for engaging in sexual activities," he said.

According to NGEC Chairperson Joyce Mwikali, the launch of the report and implementation by stakeholders will protect children below 18 years as they are not mature enough to make decisions physically, socially and psychologically.

"The launch of this report adds on to the great effort by state and non-state actors in the overall protection of children’s rights,” she expressed.

The report came into the fore two years since the Court of Appeal proposed an amendment to lower the age of consent from 18 to 16 years.

As per the court, underage girls and boys often engage in romantic relations despite not having attained maturity because they can make informed decisions about their lives and bodies.
