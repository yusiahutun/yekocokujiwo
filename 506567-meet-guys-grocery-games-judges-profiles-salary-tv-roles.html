Meet Guy's Grocery Games judges: profiles, salary, and TV roles

April 21, 2024
blog

The host determines all the cooking and shopping challenges on the reality TV show Guy's Grocery Games. But the food the talented chefs make is ultimately judged by a panel of celebrities and famous tastemakers from the food world. Food Network keenly selects the Guy's Grocery Games Judges; it only picks those with immense culinary knowledge. They are usually winners of Food Network's cooking shows, chefs, cookbook authors and restaurant owners.

Guy’s Grocery Games, famous as Triple G, is a reality cooking TV show hosted by Guy Fieri on Food Network. Each show episode features four chefs competing in a three-round elimination contest. They cook food using ingredients bought in a selected supermarket grocery store. Plus, the host, Guy Fieri, gives the chefs unusual challenges that make the show more interesting.

Guy’s Grocery Games judges

The Guy's Grocery Games TV show premiered in 2013 and has aired 256 episodes. Each Triple G episode features a panel of three judges evaluating competitor foods. So, who are the judges on Guy's Grocery Games? Here is a list of the regular judges in the food TV show. But it doesn't include the past judges on Guy's Grocery Games.

1. Duskie Estes

In seasons 3 and 5, viewers remember Duskie Estes as an incredible chef. Duskie lives in Northern California with her family and runs MacBryde Farm, Black Pig Meat Co., and Zazu Kitchen + Farm with her husband. Duskie makes regular appearances on Guy's Grocery Games as a judge. Currently, she's the judge of the special All-Stars episodes.

2. Troy Johnson

Troy is a food critic and journalist for the San Diego Magazine. He enjoys sampling food from different restaurants and writing down his experiences. Troy has appeared on Food Network shows as a judge and panelist at The Best Thing I Ever Ate and Iron Chef America, respectively.

He is best known as a regular judge on Guy's Grocery Games and previously hosted Crave on Food Network.

3. Brian Malarkey

Brian Malarkey is a restaurateur and chef who made a name in Los Angeles long before opening several restaurants in California. He also has vast TV experience; Malarkey regularly appears as a Guy's Grocery Games judge. He has also been featured in Good Morning America and the Today Show. Brian was The Taste best chef, winning mentor, and finalist.

4. Melissa d’Arabian

Melissa Donovan, one of the loved female judges on Guy's Grocery Games, is an American TV show host and cookbook author. She was the winner of season five of The Next Food Network Star.

Following her win, Melissa hosted Drop 5 Lbs. and Ten Dollar Dinners. She's also the author of two cookbooks, her recent being Supermarket Healthy. Melissa has also hosted a Web series, The Picky Eaters Project, which helps families efficiently plan mealtime. She is also an active mom of four and regularly serves as the Guy's Grocery Games judge.

5. Beau MacMillan

Beau Macmillan (nicknamed BeauMac) is an American TV personality and chef. He is the executive chef of an upscale resort in Arizona, Sanctuary on Camelback.

Bruce Seidel of Food Network invited him to Iron Chef America in 2005. Beau accepted the invite, and he defeated Bobby Flay. In 2010, Beau and Anne Burrell cohosted season one of Worst Cooks in America.

In 2011, he was featured in The Next Iron Chef but was eliminated from the competition in season four. He also featured in Guy's Grocery Games as a judge and a chef who won $16,000.

6. Catherine McCord

Catherine is an American TV program hostess, actress and former model - she went from catwalk to kitchen. After graduating from the prestigious Institute of Culinary Education, Catherine started a blog, Weelicious.com, to showcase her easy, family-friendly recipes.

She is the author of Weelicious: One Family, One Meal. When McCord, the mother of two, isn't testing recipes or writing, she's working as a judge on Guy's Grocery Games.

7. Aarti Sequeira

Aarti Lucica Sequeira is an Indian American TV personality and cook. She's famous as the season six winner of The Next Food Network Star. In 2010, after her win, Aarti premiered a show called Aarti Party on the network. It was inspired by her blog AartiPaarti.com.

She then hosted Taste in Translation, exploring the most popular and delicious meals globally. Currently, Aarti is a regular Indian judge on GGG and even competes in games in the special All-Stars series.

8. Madison Cowan

Maddison Cowan is a judge at Triple G and a competitor on the All-Stars special. The TV personality and chef rose to fame when he became the first-ever Chopped grand champion. But Madison had been cooking since childhood, so he knew these things.

9. Gerry Garvin

Chef Gerry Garvin, a regular judge at Guy's Grocery Games, is famous for his hit TV show Turn Up the Heat with G. Garvin on TV One. It ran for seven seasons.

He also has a great cookbook, Turn Up the Heat with G. Garvin, that won an American Literacy Award. Now, Garvin is a chef at Low Country, Atlanta. But before that, he was responsible for launching high restaurants in Los Angeles, where he cooked for world dignitaries and celebrities.
