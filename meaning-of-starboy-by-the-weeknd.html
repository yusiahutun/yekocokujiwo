Meaning of Starboy by The Weeknd

March 03, 2024
blog

The Weeknd's hit song "Starboy" is a compelling and introspective track that explores themes of power, success, and self-discovery. Through a combination of catchy beats and thought-provoking lyrics, the song showcases the artist's evolution, both musically and personally.The opening verse immediately sets the tone for the song's overarching theme. The lyrics "I'm tryna put you in the worst mood" and "Millipointtwo just tohurt you" suggest a desire to assert dominance and control. The theme of power dynamics is present throughout the song, as the protagonist flaunts his success and toys with those who may be envious or attempt to challenge him.Taking a closer look at the lyrics, certain standout phrases can be analyzed to draw out unexpected themes. For instance, the line "None of these toys on lease too" suggests that the starboy lives a life of ownership and autonomy. This speaks to the idea of self-sufficiency, as he doesn't rely on others for his success. Furthermore, the mention of a "Main bitch out your league too" and a "Side bitch out of your league too" introduces the theme of validation and the pursuit of desire. The starboy becomes an object of desire and symbolizes the fulfillment of both romantic and sexual fantasies.Moving on to the pre-chorus, the lyrics "House so empty, need a centerpiece" present an interesting theme of emptiness and the search for fulfillment. Despite material wealth and success, the starboy feels a void that needs to be filled. The mention of cutting "ivory into skinny pieces" and cleaning it with her face speaks to an unconventional method of finding satisfaction. This suggests that the starboy finds escapism not only through material possessions but also through unconventional pleasures.The chorus itself reinforces the concept of self-assertion and boasts the protagonist's status as a "motherfuckin' starboy." Here, the overarching theme of power and dominance is reaffirmed, emphasizing the starboy's transformation and ascent in the music industry. This theme transcends the literal interpretation of fame and success, symbolizing the reclaiming of one's identity and the emergence of a new self.The second verse introduces a sense of conflict and adversity. The lyrics "Every day a nigga try to test me" and "Every day a nigga try to end me" touch upon the concept of resilience and survival. This unexpected theme adds depth to the song, suggesting that success isn't always served on a silver platter. In order to maintain his position as a starboy and overcome challenges, the protagonist embraces the fall time as a time of rebirth and revitalization.The final verse carries the theme of self-empowerment and transformation even further. The lyrics "Legend of the fall took the year like a bandit" signify the starboy's rise to prominence and his ability to conquer obstacles in his path. The mention of buying "mama a crib and a brand new wagon" reflects his desire to provide for his loved ones and showcase his achievements. However, the line "We don't pray for love, we just pray for cars" introduces an unexpected theme of prioritizing material possessions over emotional connections. This suggests that the starboy's journey is not solely about fame but also about accumulating wealth and status.In conclusion, "Starboy" by The Weeknd embodies various themes related to power, success, and self-discovery. Through lyrics that explore dominance, self-validation, escapism, resilience, and the pursuit of wealth, the song presents an intricate narrative of personal growth and transformation. Ultimately, the track serves as an anthem for those who have faced adversity and come out stronger, asserting their dominance in the face of challenges. The Weeknd's journey as a starboy reflects many universal experiences and allows listeners to connect with the song's themes on a personal level.
