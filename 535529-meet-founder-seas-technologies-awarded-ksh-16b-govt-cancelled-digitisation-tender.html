Michael Macharia, the co-founder of SevenSeas Technology Limited, won a KSh 1.6 billion award against the government after it cancelled a KSh 4.7 billion contract to wire 98 state hospitals.

Why Michael Macharia was awarded KSh 1.6 billion

Former judge Aaron Ringera, who is currently an arbitrator, determined that the state was at fault for ending the multi-billion contract that required SevenSeas Technologies Limited to supply the technology for the Managed Equipment Service (MES) plan.

The contract was given to SevenSeas Technologies in October 2017.

One of the agreement's terms was teleradiology, which enables medical professionals at prestigious institutions like Kenyatta National Hospital (KNH) to interpret MRIs, CT scans, X-rays, and other imaging tests for treating patients in remote locations.

Business Daily reported that on November 18, 2019, the Ministry of Health sent a termination letter to the tech firm, of which Macharia owns 60%.

The letter stated that the contract had multiple requirements, including the need for a government letter of support, which was not included in the initial tender paperwork.

The government claimed that despite having invested more than $11.11 million (KSh 1.8 billion), the Kenyan IT company, valued at an estimated KSh 3.2 billion in 2016, lacked the financial resources to guide the deal.

What was Aaron Ringera's ruling?

In protesting the cancellation, SevenSeas Technologies Limited argued that the state had refused to provide a letter of support, a security document that most state projects typically include and which provides banks confidence to lend project money to businesses or individuals.

The IT company claimed that the letter of support issue complicated their application for a loan from KCB Group and led to the two-year lawsuit that highlighted the arduous process faced by entrepreneurs working on state projects.

"And I have found that the second respondent (Ministry of Health) was in breach of both payment obligations under the contract and the obligation to provide a government letter of support. Both defaults are material breaches of the second respondent's contractual obligations," Ringera ruled.

He awarded the company KSh 1.59 billion in compensation for contract violations, lost earnings, and post-cancellation expenses. An extra KSh 52 million was given to the tech company to cover suit-related expenses.

The arbitrator heard that SevenSeas Technologies requested funding and received bids, including KSh 3.68 billion in equity financing for the project. According to Ringera, this amount of money exceeded the necessary funds for the health ICT agreement.

SevenSeas was staking its future billions and relying on the health industry, particularly state transactions, to generate income.

Who is Michael Macharia?

According to his LinkedIn profile, Macharia is the founder and group chief visionary officer of Ponea, a healthcare aggregator that uses technology platforms to supply medical services to certified, licensed, and vetted healthcare providers.

He is a serial entrepreneur who, at the young age of 25, started Seven Seas Technologies, one of Kenya's IT services companies.

In addition to being the former chairman of the SME Scale-Up program and the Kenya Information Technology & Outsourcing Society (KITOS), he is a trained accountant with over 20 years of experience in entrepreneurship.
