Wearing jewelry is an effective and beautiful way to elevate any outfit but it can also be deeply personal. While some pieces in your jewelry drawer may just be fun to wear on a night out, other pieces, like a wedding band, can hold so much significance, that they almost feel like they’re a part of your very being. This significance often applies to jewelry that you wear every day. If you already believe in the power of personal jewelry, you should consider adding the Evil Eye jewelry trend to your collection—it might just make a difference in your luck. 

You may have heard the phrase, “giving someone the evil eye” which basically translates into shooting someone a dirty look. If someone gives you the “evil eye” it usually comes with malicious or envious intent. Evil Eye jewelry is meant to protect you from that. 

The origins of Evil Eye jewelry can be traced far back into history. While the story of the Evil Eye is popular in many cultures, it has a strong significance in West Asia and the Mediterranean. If you’ve ever spent time in Greece, you’ll notice that the local shops sell lots of Evil Eye jewelry pieces and decor—that’s because the symbol’s origin is believed to have been derived from Ancient Greece. 

If giving someone the “evil eye” or dirty look is bad, then why would you wear one symbolically? The eye design is supposed to combat any negative energy that may be directed towards you. Wearing an Evil Eye is a form of protection. 

If someone else gifts you a piece of Evil Eye jewelry, it means that they want to promote positive energy for you and help expel any negativity in your life. Some people think that the Evil Eye’s powers are stronger if gifted by someone else because it is reinforced by their strong positive intentions. However, Evil Eye jewelry is still powerful if you purchase it yourself—and setting your own intention can often lead to stronger results. 

Celebrities like Gigi Hadid and Meghan Markle are often seen wearing the symbol.

Evil Eye jewelry comes in a variety of colors, all with their own meanings, but the most common color you’ll find when shopping is blue. The color blue is associated with calm and tranquility and offers additional protection because it is the color of the sky.
