Art historian cum designer, Maggie Galton, demonstrates the myriad ways to wrap the Rebozo – a Mexican scarf handmade from cotton, wool or silk and worn as a shawl or carrying sling. The fabric is hand knotted, dyed and intricately woven on pedal looms in a labor-intensive process that can take up to two months.

As Executive Editorial Director of HarpersBAZAAR.com, Joyann King has ushered America's first fashion magazine into an era of digital excellence.
