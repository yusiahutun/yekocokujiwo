King Von was an American rapper, singer, and songwriter from Chicago, Illinois. He rose to fame due to his amazing rapping skills, which made him one of the most promising rappers in the music industry. His music talent led him to be signed to Lil Durk's (American rapper) record labels Empire Distribution and Only the Family.

King Von's story is a heartbreaking one. Despite experiencing a tough childhood, he had the talent and opportunity to turn his life around. But unfortunately, he got involved in criminal gang activities, which ended up costing him his life.

Early life

King Von (real name Davyon Daquan Bennett) was born in Chicago, Illinois, on August 9, 1994. He was the son of Walter 'Silk' Bennett and Natasha 'Taesha' Chambers. He had nine siblings – six half-siblings from his father's side and three siblings from his mother's side. Unfortunately, not much is known about his siblings, apart from his famous sister, social media personality Kayla B.

Growing up, King Von's father was in and out of prison for most of his childhood. As a result, he was raised mostly by his mother and alongside some of his siblings. Unfortunately, things did not improve as his father was shot in a gang-related shooting when he was 11.

Bennett attended Hyde Park Academy on the south side of Chicago. He attended the high school alongside Chicago's rapper, G Herbo, who was one of the people who inspired him to make drill music. While in high school, he was active in sports and even played baseball for the varsity team.

Bennet did not graduate from high school, as he got into trouble with the law and was incarcerated when he was 16 years. As a result, he got his GED in a juvenile detention centre. Nonetheless, he was committed to furthering his education and enrolled at South Suburban College in Holland, Illinois.

Trouble would find Bennet again after he was arrested on a first-degree murder charge, in 2014, following the shooting of college student Malcolm Stuckey. While he was later acquitted of the charges, his education was interrupted. As a result, he chose to focus on his music career.

Career

Bennett had a passion for music from a young age and started playing songs and rapping in his teens with his friends. However, it was not until he got out of prison -after being acquitted of murder – that he got serious with his music. His talent attracted the attention of Chicago rapper Lil Durk, who signed him to his record labels.

Soon after being signed, Bennett, who adopted, released the single Crazy Story, which was a breakout hit. In 2019, he released another track, Cousins, featuring Chicago rapper, JusBlow600. As his music career gained traction, he relocated to Atlanta, where he worked on his debut mixtape, Grandson Vol. 1.

King Von and Lil Durk collaborated on several tracks on his debut mix tape, and it was a success, reaching No. 75 on the Billboard 200. Soon after the first mix tape, he started working on a second project, Levon James, which he released in 2019. It featured collaborations with rappers NLE Choppa and YNW Melly.

After releasing his second mixtape, King started working on his debut studio album, Welcome to O'Block, which was released in October 2020. Unfortunately, it was his last project alive, as he died a few days after its release. Nonetheless, he had worked on several other songs – yet to be released – which were released as part of his second (posthumous) album, What It Means to Be King, released in 2022.

Was King Von married? Did he have any children? At the time of his death, the American rapper was not married. However, he had dated American rapper Asian Doll, Instagram star Queen Kema, and another woman named Mici.

King had two known children with two of his exes, a son with Kema and a daughter with Mici. However, a short time after his death, a woman named Deja Denise was rumoured to be expecting the rapper's child, with his sister, Kayla B, seeming to confirm the rumours.

Death

King was shot and killed on November 6, 2020. The shooting resulted from an argument between him (and his crew) and rival rapper Quando Rondo's crew outside of nightclub Atlanta. The argument escalated, resulting in Bennett being shot multiple times. He was evacuated to a hospital where he was treated in critical condition but passed on later in the day.

The shooting incident resulted in another person dying and six people being wounded. One of the shooting victims, Timothy Leeks, alias Lu Timm (affiliated with rapper Quando Rondo), was arrested and charged with the murder of King.
