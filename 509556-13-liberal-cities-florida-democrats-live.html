1. Miami

Miami is a liberal, pro-choice, and the second-largest city in Florida. It has a moderately large population of liberals than conservatives. During the 2020 elections, Miami residents voted for the Democratic president. However, the two parties shared almost half the votes, 53.31% favouring Democrats.

Miami residents have consistently voted for the Democratic Party since 2000, indicating that the people of Miami always lean right. Surprisingly, the Mayor of Miami has been a Republican since 2017.

2. Tallahassee

Tallahassee is a liberal city in the southern region of Florida. It is one of the few cities in Florida known for its progressive activism and pro-choice movements. Tallahassee has voted for a Democratic president in 24 out of 29 US presidential elections since 1904.

In the 2020 US elections, at least 63.3% of the residents in Tallahassee voted for the Democratic candidate. And since 2018, the Mayor of Tallahassee has been a Democrat making it one of the most liberal cities in Florida.

St Petersburg has always found itself in the middle of liberalism and conservatism. However, it remains one of the best cities in Florida for liberal democrats to live. During the 2020 US elections, St Petersburg voted for a Democratic candidate.
