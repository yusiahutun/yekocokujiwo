Dance Dance Dance's Lucy-Jo Hudson says Coronation Street star husband Alan Halsall should 'stick to

March 11, 2024

LUCY-JO Hudson turned down the chance to strut her stuff with Coronation Street star husband Alan Halsall on Dance Dance Dance quipping he should "stick to acting."

The bubbly northern lass - who also starred in the hit ITV soap as feisty Katy Harris - made her cheeky comments as she confessed the man playing lovable mechanic Tyrone Dobbs couldn't even manage to move in time to music at a wedding.

Lucy will perform in Dance Dance Dance's final on Sunday with trained dancer Rohan Pinnock-Hamilton as she goes head-to-head with EastEnders star Jonny Labey.

She insisted Alan didn't expect to be picked as her other-half on the dancefloor, despite the fact some like Jonny had picked their girlfriends and real-life partners for the role.

Speaking about her hubby's not-so-fancy-footwork Lucy told Good Morning Britain host Kate Garraway: "He's not even in time when we dance at a wedding!

"If you could see him dance you would be like 'no, please!'.

"He can't dance!"
Lucy-Jo added: "He's brilliant at acting - he should stick to that!"

Yet supportive Alan has often been seen in the crowds of the ITV show.

Meanwhile Johnny - who played Ben Mitchell in Albert Square - will be on-stage with girlfriend of a year Chrissy Brooke.

Chrissy is one step ahead having previously learned ballet, tap and street dance, she’s also been in the ensemble for iconic stage shows Wicked and Phantom of the Opera.

Lucy-Jo and Jonny made it through to the final after JLS star JB Gill was eliminated last weekend.

JB  – who competed on the ITV show alongside his wife Chloe – failed to win as many points as the other pair.

After being revealed as bottom of the scoreboard JB added: “For me to support Chloe having danced and coming back into it after such a long period away from her art and from growing up what she’s wanted to do, it’s been an absolute pleasure.

Dance, Dance, Dance airs on Sunday at 6.30pm on ITV.
