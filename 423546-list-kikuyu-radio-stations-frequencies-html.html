List of all Kikuyu radio stations in Kenya

Viewership and listenership trends have evolved to accommodate other emerging channels that have morphed from technologically advanced applications. However, radio has always remained one of the most effective ways to reach new and existing listeners.

Kenya has a plethora of community radio stations, but Kikuyu stations have stood out considering their footing in the entertainment industry. How many Kikuyu radio stations are on air currently?

1. Kigooco FM – 98.6 FM

Kigooco FM is one of the best Kikuyu radio stations in Kenya. The popular Kikuyu radio station broadcasts programs on Biblical understanding and promotes Christian values and practices in society. Kigooco FM also plays gospel music in Kikuyu. The radio station is located in Ngara, Nairobi, Aqua Plaza, next to Desai Road.

Aside from its 98.6 frequency in Nairobi, listeners can also tune in when in Nakuru on 105.0 FM, Nyeri 90.6 FM, Nanyuki 102.3 FM and Mombasa 102.3 FM.

The radio station has one of the most engaging radio presenters, including Tabitha James, Wambui Wa Njihia, Madam Charity, Wakarura Wa Nyutu, Muna Njomo, Gakui Kamau, Mwangangi Kimani, and Gasheri.

These lively presenters host different shows from morning until midnight. First, of course, they update listeners on current affairs, share words of hope, and play inspirational gospel songs. On top of this, Kigooco FM hosts guests with touching stories that inspire the audience.

2. Gukena FM – 92.2 FM

Gukena FM is owned by Radio Africa Group, the company that owns Kiss FM, Classic 105, The Star and MPASHO. The Kikuyu radio station targets urban and pre-urban, young Kikuyu speaking listeners all over Kenya. If you are outside Nairobi, you can tune in to the station on 92.8 FM in Nyeri and Nakuru.

During the radio station’s launch, the general manager, Martin Khafafa, stated that there is a lot about the county (Nyeri) and heritage that people do not know. And it is upon the radio station to share it with the world. Gukena FM is located along Ring Road, Nyeri.

3. Inooro FM – 98.9 FM

Inooro is likely the best Kikuyu radio station. According to the official website of the radio station, Inooro FM is Kenya's biggest vernacular radio station by listener attraction and audience measurement. The radio station plays 98% pure Kikuyu music and 2% African and international hits.

Inooro’s owned by Royal Media Services, one of the largest media companies in Kenya with enormous investments in the entertainment industry. The radio station's format and style cater to all the socio-economic groups across the board in Kenya.

The term Inooro means a tool for sharpening in Kikuyu vocabulary, and the radio station has done its best to create a sound reputation based on its name. If you are not in Nairobi, you can listen to the radio station on the following frequencies:

  • Central: 97.8 FM
  • Meru: 95.1 FM
  • Chuka: 102.0 FM
  • Nakuru: 88.9 FM
  • Eldoret: 107.0 FM
  • Mombasa: 92.2 FM

4. Kameme FM – 101.1 FM

Kameme FM was established in Nairobi in 2000 and is likely the oldest Kikuyu radio station in Kenya amongst the ones still on the air today. The radio station was founded by Kimotho, the Managing Director of Regional Reach Limited.

Kameme FM is currently owned by Mediamax Limited, a company associated with President Uhuru Kenyatta. Since its launch, the radio station has grown in popularity and is amongst the most known Kikuyu radio stations, even among the non-Kikuyu speaking community.

Kameme FM has popular presenters: Benson Gathungu, Man Nyari, Kamau wa Kamlesh, Githaya Denis, and Muthoni wa Kirumba. The radio station is listened to by around three million people all over Kenya.

You can listen to your favourite shows, programs, presenters, and news on the following frequencies outside Nairobi.

  • Malindi: 89.3 FM
  • Nyeri: 92.3 FM
  • Nakuru: 101.3 FM
  • Eldoret: 101.9 FM
  • Meru: 106.7 FM
  • Mombasa: 97.6 FM
  • Naivasha: 99.9 FM
  • Molo: 99.9 FM
  • Nyahururu: 99.4 FM
  • Muranga: 101.0 FM
  • Narok: 101.3 FM
  • Kisumu: 95.7 FM
  • Webuye: 106.7 FM.

5. Coro FM – 95.5 FM

The Kenya Broadcasting Corporation owns Coro FM, and it is the company's official Kikuyu vernacular radio station in Kenya.

Before Kameme and Inooro were established, Coro FM held the most significant Kikuyu radio station listenership share. However, things have changed as the new and more vibrant stations have tapped into the segment. The Kikuyu radio station is mainly listened to by older men and women.

6. Iganjo FM – 94.6 FM

If you are looking for the most vibrant Kikuyu radio station in Kenya, Ignajo FM is the ideal platform. The radio station is hardly ten years old and is listened to by many people, mainly from the Mount Kenya region.

Iganjo FM has energetic radio presenters and likens itself to a landslide as inferred by the famous catchphrase Riri ni Ituika.

7. Rware FM – 101.7 FM

Rware Media Services manage Rware Community Radio. The renowned radio station was created in 2010 and went on air in May 2012.

The radio station was previously located at Wambugu Farmers training Centre that is popularly known as Wambugu Farm. The property’s located around 12 km from Nyeri town. However, Rware FM has since relocated to Nyeri town and also broadcast outside the region.

Rware FM primarily concentrates on current affairs, entertainment, and agri-business in Nyeri County and its environs. On top of this, the radio station has programs relating to agriculture, health, business, entrepreneurship, civic education, youth affairs, spiritual nourishment, leadership, among many other local matters.

Due to many individuals speaking Kikuyu in Kenya, Kikuyu radio stations are more popular than radio stations from other tribes. You might even be surprised to find out that some of these radio stations are more popular than their sister TV companies.
