As we gear up for another edition of this annual ceremony, you may be wondering how to watch the Kennedy Center Honors live for free. This year, stars such as Queen Latifah, Dionne Warwick, and Bee Gees’ legendary singer Barry Gibb will be recognized for their impact on American culture. Up ahead, we’re diving into how you can catch all the action—from the award ceremony, to the performances honoring these luminaries—without even breaking the bank.

Originating in 1978, the Kennedy Center Honors is a yearly ceremony honoring individuals in the performing arts and showcasing their lifetime achievements. The grand gala, traditionally held in December at the Kennedy Center Opera House in Washington D.C., is known for its star-studded attendees. In 2021, President Joe Biden and First Lady Jill Biden attended the gala, marking the first time since 2016 that a sitting president and first lady attended the event.

While the awards show isn’t broadcast live, an edited version of the event typically airs on CBS around Christmas. The honorees are selected based on recommendations from the general public, former Honorees, and the Kennedy Center Board of Trustees. The Center says the recipients have “each had an impact on the rich tapestry of American life and culture through the performing arts.”

Over the years, artists like Cher, The Eagles, Al Pacino, Lin-Manuel Miranda, Carole King, Tom Hanks, and more have graced the Kennedy Center stage. Now, with Queen Latifah, Dionne Warwick, and Barry Gibb taking the spotlight, you might be wondering where and how you can watch the Kennedy Center Honors live for free this year.
