Medical engineering is a field that has been around for decades, but it continues to transform and evolve. Combining the design and problem-solving skills of engineering with medical sciences, this field of study seeks to advance health care treatment, including diagnosis, monitoring and therapy.

What is medical engineering?

Hospitals use an increasingly wide range of medical equipment, from simple devices like nebulisers to sophisticated technologies such as radiotherapy linear accelerators for cancer treatments. All the equipment must work correctly and be safe to use. This is where medical engineering comes in.

It is the application of engineering principles in the exploration and development of medical applications and treatment technologies associated with severe or chronic medical illnesses.

Biomedical engineers study, design, develop and evaluate biological and medical systems and products such as artificial organs, prostheses, medical instruments and information systems. They can work in different places such as research companies, manufacturing companies, government agencies, and medical centres.

Critical qualities of a medical engineer

Soft skills are critical because medical engineers need to be able to work with many different professions and personality types in the healthcare and research fields. Below are the qualities of a successful engineer.

  • Great analytical skills: A medical engineer must have the ability to analyse the needs of clients and patients in order to make proper designs.
  • Good communication skills: Medical engineers often find themselves interacting with patients. Therefore, it is important for them to have excellent communication skills to express themselves in a clear way. They also need to have good listening abilities and be able to incorporate other ideas into the process of solving problems.
  • Creativity: Every medical engineer must be innovative enough to come up with creative and intelligent progress in healthcare devices and apparatus.
  • Excellent mathematical skills: Medical engineers always apply the principles of calculus and other radical subjects in mathematics and statistics for the proper making of analysis, designing, and troubleshooting in their work.
  • Problem-solving skills: Medical engineers basically deal with and tackle problems in complicated biological systems.

What are the roles of medical engineers?

Much like every field of engineering, medical engineering covers a wide range of career paths and opportunities. The duties and roles of medical engineers vary from one position to another. In general, biomedical engineering roles include:

  • To conduct research on engineering aspects of the biological systems of both humans and animals in conjunction with life scientists, chemists and medical scientists.
  • To install, maintain, make repairs, and provide technical support for any medical machine.
  • Prepare plans, note down technical reports, make public study papers, and give recommendations on the basis of their findings.
  • Give training to the clinicians and other staff on the proper utilisation of biomedical machines.
  • Make evaluations on the safety, efficiency and effectiveness of the medical machine.
  • To develop, run tests and evaluate medical equipment.
  • Advise on any new biomedical procurement for hospitals and medical centres.
  • Medical engineers may focus entirely on medical devices, sports medicine, stem cell studies, genomics, nanotechnology, biomarkers and much more.

How long does it take to study medical engineering?

A diploma in medical engineering takes a duration of two years. The average study duration for a Bachelor of Science in Medical Engineering is five years. However, the majority of medical engineering jobs require a master's or PhD in the same field.

Pursuing a Masters alone may take up to two years, while a PhD takes a minimum of seven years beyond the bachelor's degree program. Medical engineering covers a range of topics, including technology, medical equipment and diagnostics, modelling of computers, examination, biology, chemistry and physics.

Speciality areas for medical engineers include biomechanics, bioinstrumentation, clinical engineering, rehabilitation engineering, system physiology, cellular and tissue engineering, genomics and genetics engineering, therapeutic engineering, and so much more.

Medical engineering salary in Kenya

Professionals in Kenya receive salaries based on the marketability and the number of professionals in the particular field. Careers such as medical engineering pay well because their services are always in high demand.

So how much does a medical engineer earn in Kenya? A medical engineer earns Kshs. 123,000 on average. The salary ranges from Kshs. 54,000 to Kshs. 195,000. It is inclusive of housing, transport, and other benefits.
