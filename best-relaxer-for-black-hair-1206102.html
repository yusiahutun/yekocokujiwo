How to Select the Best Relaxer for Black Hair?

Choosing the best relaxer for black hair can be a great deal for those who haven’t done it before. Let this buying guide help you how to select the best product and be aware of the things to keep in mind—

a) Ingredients- The foremost thing you must consider is whether it contains lye or not. Lye is something that is really harmful to your scalp and not at all forgiving. Other than these, natural moisturizing properties like shea butter, olive oil, coconut oil, and honey are of great help to restore hydration during the process. These organic components are great options for ones with dry and brittle hair.

b) Formula- You must consider your scalp health before choosing the product. If you have certain skin conditions like eczema, dryness, flaky scalp, and sensitivity, it is best to go with a relaxer that is specially built for a sensitive scalp.

c) Products included - While most kits come with a relaxer and an activator, some of them offer everything all together. Other than that, some of the kits may contain neutralizing shampoo and moisturizing conditioner as additional products in the kit for you to have a hassle-free experience.

d) Strength- Not every relaxer is built for every hair type. People with fine hair should go for a mild relaxer, and coarse hair should go for a strong relaxer.

    How to Use a Relaxer for Black Hair?

    Using a hair relaxer and getting an effective result cannot be easier than this! When you have the perfect product according to your hair in your hands, you just need to follow the steps below—

    a) First, you need to check whether you are allergic to the component by conducting a patch test on your skin and hair.

    b) Secondly, you need to make sure that you don’t have any irritation or burn or any certain skin condition, to begin with.

    c) Now, use the protector that comes with the relaxer around your hairline. If it doesn’t have a protector, use petroleum jelly instead including your neck, back, forehead, and ear.

    d) Wear gloves before you mix the relaxer and the activator.

    e) Use the mixture by sectioning your hair for better results.

    Pro Tip

    Choose a quality relaxer, follow instructions precisely, and prioritize deep conditioning. Healthy, beautiful hair starts with proper care.

    f) Lastly, wash your hair thoroughly before using the neutralizing shampoo followed by the hydrating conditioner.

    FAQ's

    What is the best relaxer for black hair?

    Any relaxer without lye, which is known for damaging the hair, is the best for black hair to make it manageable and smooth. One such product is— ORS Olive Oil Hair Relaxer.

    What is the least damaging hair relaxer?

    A relaxer without lye or a perm with petroleum is known for being the least damaging hair relaxer for black hair. Other than these, a product loaded with natural ingredients is also safe and causes the least damage to your hair.

    What is the best box relaxer for black hair?

    There are so many, to be honest. Namely, Profectiv Mega Growth Hair Relaxer, Softsheen Carson Precise Relaxer, and Affirm Relaxer Kit—these are proven to be a few of the best box relaxers for black hair.

    Does a relaxer stop hair growth?

    Well, actually no! Relaxers are good for maintaining your hair texture and if you follow the procedure correctly and opt for precautionary measures, it has nothing to do with hair growth.

    How often should a black woman relax her hair?

    According to experts, it is advisable to use it every 8-10 weeks for touch-ups.

    Can relaxers damage your hair?

    Hair relaxers help your hair by making it more manageable. Some relaxers could damage your hair as they can have more harmful strong ingredients. However, relaxers with moisturizing and natural properties do more good than harm!
